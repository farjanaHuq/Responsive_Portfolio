About Me

The first camera I bought when I was a sophomore, that was a Sony point shoot camera. I mostly used to take photos of my friends, kids and nature at that time in Bangladesh. The first DSLR camera was my Canon 500D 18-55mm f 3.5-5.6 lens, which was a gift from my mom as a first anniversary gift on 2011. But I slowly started learning about photography right after my first child was born on 2014. Currently I am playing with Canon 6D, 50 mm f 1.4 lens, and I love it.

The book that I admired most about photography is “How to photograph almost everything” by Tom Ang. This is a very good book where almost everything about photography is described very precisely and clearly. However, I love the work of Steve McCurry, and Art Wolfe. I take Dave’s class on photography, it’s a room full of a wonderful teacher, and so many beautiful people! Currently I am a stay at home mom, so I took the photography class to give myself a room to keep something that I love. Dave’s class has just opened my door.

I find the most alluring thing about photography is the art of capturing a simple object extraordinarily. To me the most challenging part about photography is finding the correct light and perspective in a photo. The other challenge I feel when I take photos of kids, since they aren’t always willing to take photos. But I always like to capture them as they are. I love all their actions. I love to take photos of kids.
